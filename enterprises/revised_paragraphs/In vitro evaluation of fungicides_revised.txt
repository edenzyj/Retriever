In Vitro Evaluation of Fungicides Against Colletotrichum acutatum from Strawberry
This study evaluates the efficacy of various fungicides against Colletotrichum acutatum, a fungal pathogen affecting strawberries. Conducted in vitro, the research aimed to identify effective treatments to manage this pathogen. The results indicated varying levels of effectiveness among the tested fungicides, highlighting the importance of selecting appropriate chemical controls to protect strawberry crops from disease.
The author, James Lamondia, affiliated with the Connecticut Agricultural Experiment Station, has made significant contributions to the understanding of plant pathogens and their management strategies. This publication has been cited multiple times, demonstrating its relevance in agricultural research and disease management practices in strawberry cultivation.
